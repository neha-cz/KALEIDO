# KALEIDO

Simulating psychedelic cognition in LLM inference. 

## Mech Interp Findings

We tested whether the entropic brain hypothesis (EBH) — which holds that psychedelic-like cognitive states correspond to elevated entropy of neural dynamics — maps onto transformer inference, using attention inverse-temperature β as the interventional analog of landscape flattening under the modern Hopfield interpretation of attention. Flattening β on early layers (2–3) of Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct produced a large, reliable behavioral effect: local coherence collapsed (perplexity rose from ≈3.7 to ≈19.5) while the model's output remained non-degenerate, consistent with a "loosened" generative regime. However, a sequence of mechanistic interventions showed that this effect is not mediated by an increase in entropy. A necessity/sufficiency dissociation found that a structured perturbation of the attention output (value re-weighting), applied with attention-weight entropy held fixed at baseline, reproduced roughly three-quarters of β's behavioral effect, whereas raising attention-weight entropy while restoring the output toward baseline reproduced almost none (~5%). The behavioral effect is therefore carried by structured value-mixing propagating through the residual stream, with attention-weight entropy a co-occurring bystander rather than the causal driver.

We then asked whether a different entropy index — one computed on the residual stream rather than the attention weights — might be the correct EBH analog, since the value-mixing result predicts the disturbance lives in the activations. We screened two measures drawn from the EBH literature: Lempel-Ziv complexity (LZc) of binarized activation trajectories and a spatial-complexity measure (across-unit activation entropy), the latter being the more robust of the two as it requires no temporal-axis assumption. Neither tracked the behavioral effect. Across both the intervened layers and the network-wide mean, β's large coherence collapse was accompanied by negligible movement in spatial entropy (≈0.0–0.16% change) and a small decrease in LZc (≈4–8%). 

In other words, three distinct entropy/complexity indices — attention-weight entropy, residual-stream LZc, and residual-stream spatial entropy — all failed to rise with the behavioral effect, and the only measure that moved did so in the direction of lower complexity, consistent with β degrading coherence toward mildly more repetitive output rather than expanding the model's representational repertoire.
